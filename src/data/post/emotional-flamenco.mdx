Flamenco is known for its raw emotional intensity — the Spanish call it _duende_, that moment when art transcends technique and becomes pure feeling. This project captures **EEG data from flamenco dancers** to understand how this intense emotion manifests in brain activity.

## The Research Questions

- What neural patterns emerge during moments of high emotional expression?
- How does the brain of a flamenco dancer differ during technical vs. emotional passages?
- Can we identify the neural signature of _duende_?

## Methodology

Using mobile EEG technology, we record brain activity from flamenco dancers as they perform:

- **Zapateado** — Rhythmic footwork
- **Braceo** — Arm movements expressing emotion
- **Llamada** — Calls between dancer and musician
- **Emotional crescendos** — Moments of peak intensity

## Cultural Context

Flamenco originated in Andalusia, Spain, born from a fusion of Romani, Moorish, and Spanish cultures. Its emotional depth makes it the perfect subject for studying how cultural art forms engage the brain.

## Goals

This research aims to:

1. Document neural correlates of emotional expression in dance
2. Understand cross-cultural patterns in artistic emotion
3. Contribute to the broader "brain-art map" for potential therapeutic applications

## My Role

I am responsible for setting up and monitoring the EEG recordings during flamenco performances, ensuring high-quality data collection in challenging artistic environments.

## Timeline

Data collection took place from **May to June 2025**.
