The NeuroExo project develops brain-machine interfaces and robotic exoskeletons for rehabilitation. The latest breakthrough is **MyoStep** — the first wearable pediatric soft exoskeleton made of smart materials, designed to help children with cerebral palsy walk.

## MyoStep: A Breakthrough for Children

Cerebral palsy affects 1 to 4 out of every 1,000 births worldwide, severely impacting motor skills and mobility. Traditional exoskeletons are too heavy and don't accommodate a child's growth. MyoStep changes everything:

- **Lightweight and discreet** — looks like a soft power suit
- **Smart materials** — artificial muscles and smart fabrics
- **Growth-adaptive** — adjusts as children grow
- **Wireless sensor network** — provides real-time movement feedback

## Brain-Controlled Rehabilitation

The NeuroExo system also includes a portable brain-computer interface (BCI) exoskeleton for stroke rehabilitation. This device:

- Requires no surgery
- Can be used both in clinic and at home
- Uses EEG to decode movement intent
- Guides robotic assistance based on brain signals
